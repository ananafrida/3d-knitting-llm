      Free For All Cowl

      by Jen Peck
      Webster Street Knittery
      Knitting
      Neck / Torso → Cowl
      April 2019
      Plymouth Yarn Encore Tweed
      Sea Change Fibers Ecola Worsted
      Worsted (9 wpi) ?
      US 8 - 5.0 mm
      200 - 225 yards (183 - 206 m)
      One size - approx. 9" wide by 24" circumference - easily customizable

      This pattern is available as a free Ravelry download

      The FREE FOR ALL cowl is designed to make the most of one skein of worsted weight yarn (200-225 yards) or one skein of fingering weight yarn (400-463 yards, held double). It features an intuitive textured stripe pattern, knit-on I-cord edge for structure and a cozy, throw-on-and-go style. This cowl begins with a provisional cast on and uses Kitchener Stitch to join for a smooth, invisible finish.

      SIZES: One size, easily customized

      MEASUREMENTS
      Approximately 9 wide by 24 in circumference

      YARN REQUIREMENTS
      Worsted weight yarn approximately 200-225 yards.

      Alternatively, you can use one skein of Fingering weight yarn (400-463 yards) held double.

      Yellow sample uses The Dye Project Ecola Merino/Silk Worsted in Kolsch (85% merino, 15% silk; 215 yards per 100 grams).

      Beige sample uses Plymouth Yarn Co. Encore Tweed in Oatmeal (75% acrylic, 22% wool, 3% viscose; 200 yards per 100 grams).

      NEEDLES
      Suggested 5.0 mm (US 8) circular needles at least 16 long

      NOTIONS
      Stitch markers, waste yarn, tapestry needle, blocking materials

      GAUGE
      Gauge is not vital for this pattern. Choose a yarn and needle combination that creates a fabric that you like, keeping in mind that this may result in different yardage requirements and garment measurements.

      If you would like to support me as a designer you can do so via: https://ko-fi.com/websterstreetknittery
      Thank you!
